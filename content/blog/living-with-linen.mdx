Linen is a slow fabric. It rewards patience.

## Why we keep coming back to linen

Linen is woven from flax, a crop that needs little water and almost no
pesticide. It softens with every wash, holds dye gently, and wrinkles
honestly. A good linen throw will outlast three sofas.

## How to choose linen worth keeping

Look for stonewashed weights between 160 and 240 GSM, garment-dyed in
small batches. Avoid blends — even ten percent polyester removes the
behavior that makes linen worth buying.

## How to wash it without ruining it

Cool wash, low spin, line dry in shade. Iron only the parts people see.
Everything else can stay creased.
